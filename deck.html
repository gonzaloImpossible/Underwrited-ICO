ICO Underwriting

A structured underwriting product that gives retail ICO investors a guaranteed refund right — while creating a risk/reward opportunity for sophisticated market participants.

Market Makers Crypto Funds Structured Products
The Problem

ICOs are broken for everyone

Retail investors
Buy at the ICO price with zero downside protection. If the token dumps immediately, they have no recourse. Trust in the project team is required but rarely warranted.
Zero protection
Projects
Credibility is cheap to fake. Projects cannot credibly signal quality or long-term commitment to the market. High-quality projects are priced the same as low-quality ones.
Adverse selection
The market
ICO tokens routinely dump on listing because early buyers are incentivised to exit at first liquidity. Projects retain capital only by preventing exits — which further erodes trust.
Reflexive sell pressure
The Solution

Underwritten ICOs

A third-party underwriter commits, upfront, to purchase any tokens that investors choose to return during a defined refund window — at a pre-agreed floor price below the ICO price.

Investors get a real, funded refund right. Projects get a credibility signal that separates them from low-quality issuers. Underwriters get paid a negotiated premium and can acquire tokens at a discount to ICO price if refunds materialise.

The core trade

The underwriter is selling a funded put option to ICO investors: strike = ICO price, settlement = cash, expiry = end of refund window. The premium is paid by the project. The underwriter's downside is owning tokens at a discount to ICO price — a position they are already equipped to manage.

Underwriter P&L =
+ Premium received
− max(0, floor_price − market_price) × refunded_tokens
Mechanism

How it works — step by step

T₀
Deal signed
Project and underwriter agree on ICO price, floor price, premium, and refund window length.
T₁
ICO opens
Tokens sold at fixed price. Raised funds held in contract. Premium transferred to underwriter from raise proceeds.
T₂
Refund window
Investors may return tokens at the ICO price (100% refund) within the window — typically 7 days post-close.
T₃
Settlement
Refunded tokens sold to underwriter at the floor price. Remaining raise funds released to project. Underwriter holds refunded tokens.
T₄
Post-settlement
Underwriter manages token position on the open market. Project operates with retained capital.

The refund right is fully funded: the smart contract holds ICO proceeds in escrow during the refund window. No counterparty risk to investors. Underwriter capital is committed at signing and locked until settlement.

Underwriter Economics

Risk / reward profile

Upside cases

  • ↑ Token performs — zero refunds, collect full premium with no capital deployment. Pure yield on committed (but unused) capital.
  • ↑ Partial refunds + recovery — acquire tokens at $0.70, token recovers above floor → capital gain on position + premium.
  • ↑ Market maker synergy — underwriters with existing liquidity desks can offset token exposure through hedges or inventory management.

Downside cases

  • ↓ Token collapses — high refund rate + token price below floor. Premium provides a buffer; max loss = (floor − market) × refunded tokens − premium.
  • ↓ Illiquid token — refunded token position is hard to exit. Mitigated by exchange listing commitments at deal sign.
Break-even token price
$0.65
On a 100% refund with 5% premium at $0.70 floor
Premium absorbs the first $0.05 of price decline below floor on a full-refund scenario. Each additional $0.10 decline = ~$1M loss per $10M raise.
Capital efficiency
Up to 7.1%
Premium yield on max committed capital (no refunds)
If zero refunds occur, the committed $7M was never deployed. The $500K premium = 7.1% yield on peak capital commitment with zero deployment.

Risk Management

Key risks & mitigations

Adverse selection
Projects with weak fundamentals self-select into the product, expecting high refund rates and needing the capital floor.
→ Underwriter DD is the gate. Only underwrite projects where you have conviction on fundamentals and team. Premium must reflect quality signal.
Coordinated refund attack
Organised buyers could purchase the ICO solely to trigger 100% refunds, forcing maximum token deployment at floor price.
→ Per-wallet refund caps, KYC gates, and whitelist mechanisms limit attack surface. Refund window is short (7 days) limiting coordination time.
Token illiquidity
Post-settlement, underwriter holds a large token position in a potentially illiquid market with no guaranteed exit.
→ Deal terms include exchange listing commitments and minimum liquidity requirements. Underwriter can negotiate lock-waiver or OTC desk rights.
Smart contract risk
Escrow and settlement logic must be audited. A contract bug could misallocate raised funds or fail to execute refunds.
→ All contracts audited by two independent firms before launch. Underwriter can require audit completion as a condition of signing.
Strategic Fit

Why this is a natural fit for market makers

Existing infrastructure
Market makers already run token inventory desks, OTC books, and hedging operations. Absorbing refunded tokens at a discount is an extension of existing workflows — not a new capability.
Deal flow advantage
Underwriting the ICO puts you closest to the project at day zero — before the token is listed. You build a relationship, gain data on refund rates, and can structure MM agreements off the back of the underwrite.
Premium as carry
On zero-refund deals, the premium is pure yield on committed capital that was never deployed. A portfolio of underwriting positions generates carry while the underlying capital remains available.

The analogy in TradFi is clear: this is IPO stabilisation / greenshoe mechanics ported to token markets. The difference is that here the protection is explicit, pre-agreed, and structurally enforced — not a discretionary aftermarket intervention. Sophisticated desks already understand this payoff profile.

Process

  • ① Due diligence — project submits materials; underwriter reviews team, tokenomics, use-of-funds, legal.
  • ② Term sheet — floor price, premium, window, caps agreed and signed.
  • ③ Capital lock — underwriter commits floor-price capital to escrow or provides LoC ahead of ICO launch.
  • ④ ICO + window — platform manages refunds; underwriter receives daily refund data.
  • ⑤ Settlement — refunded tokens and capital exchanged atomically on-chain.
