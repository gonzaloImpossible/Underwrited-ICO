The Problem

Why token sales fail — for everyone

Investors take all the risk
Funds are sent on day one. The team has the capital before delivering anything. Investors have no recourse if the team underdelivers, pivots, or disappears.
Rug risk
TGE creates sell pressure
Tokens are liquid at launch. Early buyers are incentivised to sell immediately, before insiders and others dump. The result is a race to exit that destroys price.
Reflexive dump
Teams have no accountability
Once funds are received, the team faces no financial consequence for under-execution. The incentive to keep building weakens as the runway is secured.
Misaligned incentives

The root cause is simple: capital transfer is decoupled from performance. The Investor-Protected ICO fixes this by keeping funds in escrow and releasing them to the team only as trust is established over time.

The Solution

Capital earned, not taken

Raised funds are held in escrow managed by Impossible Finance and released to the team in equal tranches over 10 months — one tranche per month, contingent on no mass investor exit.

Investors receive their purchased tokens gradually as airdrops each month. For as long as they hold unclaimed tokens, they retain a refund right that declines symmetrically with each tranche released to the team.

At any point, an investor can claim all remaining tokens (giving up the refund right) or refund (returning unclaimed tokens for the corresponding escrow balance). The choice is always theirs.

The key invariant
At every moment in time:

Escrow balance = Total outstanding refund rights

The contract is always solvent. Every investor who wants a refund can get one, regardless of how many others also request refunds simultaneously.
Refund right(month M) = 100% − M × 10%
Escrow balance(month M) = Raise × (1 − M/10)

∴ Escrow balance = Refund right × Raise ✓
Mechanism

What happens at each stage

At ICO close / TGE
Funds locked in escrow. Tokens minted and allocated but not yet distributed. Investors may claim all tokens immediately — but doing so permanently waives the refund right. Most rational investors will hold.
Every month (months 1–10)
Three things happen simultaneously:

① Token airdrop — investors who have not claimed NOR refunded receive 10% of their purchased tokens automatically.

② Escrow release — 10% of the raised amount is transferred to the team.

③ Refund settlement — investors who opted for a refund during the month receive their protected funds.
Investor choices (anytime)
At any point between monthly settlements an investor can:

Hold — do nothing. Receive the next airdrop, refund right decreases by 10%.

Claim — receive all remaining unissued tokens immediately. Refund right permanently waived.

Refund — return unclaimed token allocation, receive current refund right amount from escrow.
After month 10
All tokens have been airdropped. Escrow is empty. Refund right = 0. The project is fully funded and all token obligations are settled.

Market Dynamics

Why TGE looks different

Standard ICO at TGE

  • → Early buyers paid $0.10 in seed rounds, ICO price $1.00 — 10× paper gain incentivises immediate exit
  • → All tokens unlocked at once; only coordination (lock-ups) holds supply back
  • → Retail FOMO buyers absorb sell pressure from insiders and ICO participants
  • → Price decline triggers more exits → reflexive spiral

Investor-Protected ICO at TGE

  • → Claiming tokens at TGE forfeits 100% refund right — rational investors wait
  • → Only investors with strong conviction (or distress) claim and sell immediately
  • → Reduced supply pressure at launch supports price — which further reduces incentive to exit
  • → Virtuous cycle: strong price → investors hold → refund rate falls → team delivers
The optionality premium
The refund right has real monetary value. An investor with a 90% refund right on a $1,000 position has $900 of downside protection. Selling their tokens at TGE for $1,000 market price is only better if they believe the token will fall below ~$0.10 (their airdrop value after 1 month).

Most investors will hold if the project is credible — the refund right is worth more than the price discount from selling early.

The protection structure turns ICO investors from short-term traders into option-holders who are incentivised to wait and observe. The supply that would have dumped at TGE is held back by rational self-interest, not lock-up contracts.
